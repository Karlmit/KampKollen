## Design Context

### Users
Office colleagues using the app during or around a company sports event (5-kamp / pentathlon). Mix of tech-savvy and casual users. Used on mobile phones in a chaotic, group setting — standing around tables, cheering, competitive energy. Also checked on phones before and after the event for standings.

### Brand Personality
**Energetic · Playful · Bold** — "Sports broadcast for your office." The interface should make a Friday afternoon competition feel like a real sports event: big numbers, live feel, competitive hierarchy.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light background, dark green (`#0c4433`) as primary brand color
- **Typography**: LL Brown Black (display/UI), LL Brown (body) — Liseberg design system
- **Tone**: ESPN scoreboard meets office party — bold numbers, live indicators, rank drama
- **Reference feel**: Live sports scoreboard app, not an admin dashboard
- **Anti-reference**: Generic enterprise SaaS, clinical whitespace, flat icon grids

### Design Principles
1. **Numbers first** — scores and rankings dominate visually. Big, bold, unmissable.
2. **Live energy** — when active, the UI feels alive: pulsing dot, LIVE badge, #1 team gets gold treatment.
3. **Rank hierarchy matters** — #1 looks dramatically different from #4.
4. **Mobile-first, one-thumb** — generous touch targets, satisfying press feedback.
5. **Consistent craft** — same section header style, same arrow style, same spacing rhythm everywhere.

### Quality Bar
Flagship — sweat every detail. Should look polished enough to screenshot and share in the group chat.
